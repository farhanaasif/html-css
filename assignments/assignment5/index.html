My Favorite Book

Lean In: Women, Work, and the Will to Lead
Written by Sheryl Sandberg.

Sandberg is the chief operating officer of Facebook and is ranked on Fortune’s list of the 50 Most Powerful Women in Business. She is Hovard graduate and one of most inspiring women in technology. She was the only woman in her class to win a Harvard Business School award.

In Lean In, Sheryl Sandberg examines why women’s progress in achieving leadership roles has stalled, explains the root causes, and offers compelling, commonsense solutions that can empower women to achieve their full potential.

Sandberg advise women on how to find a supportive spouse and encourages women to act more like men

I don't really agree to her thought to my 100% but I learn a lot on how I can inspire others in leadership role and progress my career in IT.

Why I like this book?

In my opinion, Sandberg want women to be tough push themselves ahead to progress in their career even when they have young children. I don't really agree with her on this point and not everyone has a supportive spouse and family like her. Yet, there is are whole lot thing to learn from her experience and wisdom.

  • She is very right about why women’s progress in achieving leadership roles has stalled.
  • Her point that Women unintentionally hold themselves back in their careers.
  • She recounts her own decisions, mistakes, and daily struggles to state her point.
  • Her humor and wisdom.
  • She provides practical advice on negotiation techniques.
  • She has a lot to learn and inspire in the book.

Accomplishments of Sheryl Sandberg

1. Calculating Values With calc()

calc() function. It allows to do simple arithmetic calculations inside CSS. I used to determined the div widths while I was working on responsive layouts of this assignment to place contents correctly.

Example: width: calc(100% - 40px);

Browser Support: It is supported by all browsers except Opera 8.0. and IE 8.0.

Importing Awesome fonts

We can add beautiful fonts by simply including a style-sheet in our page. I am importing fonts in my style-sheet.

Example: Add link of the font on top of style sheet like this:

@import url("font-awesome.min.css");

@import url("http://fonts.googleapis.com/css?family=Lato:400,400italic,700,700italic|Source+Code+Pro:400");

We can simple use font like we normally do :

font-family: "Lato", sans-serif;

Browser Suport: It works well on all browsers including old versions of IE.
